Problem Statement 1

The global chocolate market is evolving, shaped by diverse consumption patterns that reflect cultural preferences, economic conditions, and strategic marketing. Despite the global pandemic, the per capita consumption of chocolate saw an increase from 2019 to 2020, signaling a resilient and growing demand. Specifically, consumption rates rose from a range of 120 grams to 8.8 kilograms per person in 2019 to between 140 grams and 9.2 kilograms in 2020. This growth trajectory is set to continue, with market forecasts predicting an expansion from $137.599 billion in 2019 to an estimated $182.090 billion by 2025, achieving a CAGR of 4.78%. To capitalize on this opportunity, chocolate manufacturers are innovating by diversifying flavors and offering premium, organic options, highlighting the need for strategic differentiation in a competitive market.
